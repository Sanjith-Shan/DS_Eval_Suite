### Justification: Simpson's Paradox
This dataset is a classic example of **Simpson's Paradox**. While Treatment B appears more effective in the aggregate, Treatment A is superior in every subgroup (both mild and severe). The aggregate data is misleading because of a confounding variable: **severity**.

Treatment A was disproportionately assigned to **severe cases** (263 out of 350, or 75%), which have a much lower baseline success rate. Conversely, Treatment B was mostly assigned to **mild cases** (270 out of 350, or 77%), which are much easier to treat. This imbalance creates an illusion of superiority for Treatment B in the overall totals, but when comparing like-for-like patients, Treatment A consistently delivers better outcomes.
