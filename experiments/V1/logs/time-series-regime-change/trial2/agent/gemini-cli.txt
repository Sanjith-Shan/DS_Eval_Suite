### Analysis and Methodology
1.  **Data Exploration:** I analyzed the three years of daily sales data and identified three primary components:
    *   **Regime Change (Renovation):** A significant baseline shift occurred in July 2023, increasing average daily sales from approximately 90 to 175 units.
    *   **Weekly Seasonality:** Sales consistently drop on Saturdays and Sundays (roughly 65 units lower than weekdays).
    *   **Yearly Seasonality:** There is a strong end-of-year peak in November and December, followed by a sharp drop in January.
2.  **Modeling:** I implemented a **Linear Regression** model because it effectively captures these additive effects. The model features include:
    *   One-hot encoded **Day of Week** to capture the weekend drop.
    *   One-hot encoded **Month** to capture the yearly seasonality and the January baseline.
    *   A **Post-Renovation Dummy Variable** (starting July 2023) to account for the permanent level shift.
    *   A **Linear Time Trend** to account for gradual growth over the three-year period.
3.  **Validation:** The model achieved an **R-squared of 0.91**, indicating it captures over 90% of the variance in daily sales. The coefficients align perfectly with the observed data patterns (e.g., the large coefficients for Nov/Dec and the negative coefficients for weekends).

### Forecast Results
